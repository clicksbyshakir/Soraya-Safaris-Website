## 1. Project goal

Build a warm, refined, high-trust website for Soraya Safaris: a Kenya-focused safari company that curates thoughtful journeys for travelers from the United States and Canada. The website must create emotion, demonstrate local credibility, make planning feel clear, and convert visitors into qualified safari inquiries.

**Initial release:** an editorial, mobile-first marketing site with inquiry capture, not a full live-booking engine.

**Primary conversion:** submit a detailed “Plan My Safari” inquiry.  
**Secondary conversion:** request a call or contact Soraya by email/WhatsApp.  
**Initial language:** English.  
**Primary destination:** Kenya.

## 2. Who we are

Soraya Safaris is a Kenya-focused safari company founded by three friends with deep personal ties to the country. We curate private and small-group journeys to Kenya’s most memorable wildlife regions, combining local knowledge in Nairobi with personal support for travelers in the United States and Canada.

- **Abdul — Nairobi, Kenya:** born in Kenya and based in Nairobi, providing an active local presence.
- **Huzaifa — Boston, United States:** born in Kenya and now based in Boston, connecting Kenyan roots with the U.S. traveler perspective.
- **Murtaza — Ottawa, Canada:** based in Ottawa and raised in Kenya from age eleven to twenty, bringing both a Kenyan upbringing and a Canadian traveler perspective.

**Founder positioning:** *Three friends. Three cities. One home in Kenya.*

## 3. Why Soraya exists

Many travelers are overwhelmed by unfamiliar park names, seasonal differences, lodge choices, transfers, internal flights, and conflicting online advice. Soraya exists to make those decisions understandable and to turn them into a journey that feels coherent, personal, and rooted in Kenya.

We listen first, explain choices clearly, recommend what fits the guest rather than what is easiest to sell, and remain accessible throughout the journey.

## 4. Meaning of the name

### Approved master brand story

Soraya Safaris takes its name from the Pleiades — the bright cluster of stars known across the Swahili coast and the Arab world as Soraya. For generations, those stars guided travelers home and told farmers when the season had turned. That is the spirit of what we do: Kenya is home to us, and we plan every journey the way you would plan one for family — with people who are actually from here, who know the land, and who stay with you from the first idea to the last night under the stars.

### Meaning pillars

- **Guidance:** clear advice from first idea to final night.
- **Home:** Kenyan roots, relationships, and family-level care.
- **Season:** awareness that weather, wildlife, and landscape shape a journey.
- **Discovery:** curiosity-led itineraries instead of fixed packages.
- **Stewardship:** respect for wildlife, communities, guides, and landscapes.

## 6. Mission, vision, and positioning

### Mission

To help travelers from the United States and Canada experience Kenya through thoughtful, personally curated safaris grounded in local knowledge, clear communication, and genuine care.

### Vision

To become a trusted Kenya-focused safari partner for North American travelers: known for personal planning, honest guidance, strong local relationships, and journeys that feel unmistakably connected to place.

### Positioning

For travelers in the United States and Canada who want a meaningful Kenya safari without the confusion of planning it alone, Soraya Safaris provides tailored itinerary design and personal support from people with real ties to Kenya. Unlike generic global travel marketplaces, Soraya is Kenya-specific, locally grounded, and involved throughout the planning journey.

## 7. Audience

Primary customers are first-time safari guests, couples, families, friends, and small private groups in the United States and Canada. They value expert help, trustworthy communication, good pacing, and a journey that feels personal.

The website must answer:

- Why should I trust Soraya?
- What makes each destination different?
- How does planning work?
- Can the journey fit my dates, group, pace, and comfort level?
- Who supports me before and during the safari?
- What is included and when will I receive a price?
- How can I begin without committing immediately?

### Personality

Warm, assured, observant, refined, and deeply rooted. Never flashy, generic, loud, over-scripted, or pretentious.

### Photography

Use Kenya-specific landscapes, people, guides, atmosphere, and wildlife. Favor golden hour and blue hour, wide frames, natural moments, and real texture. Avoid exaggerated orange filters, staged luxury clichés, community exoticism, and a site composed only of predator close-ups.

Suggested image balance: 60% place and atmosphere, 25% people and hospitality, 15% wildlife detail.

## 9. Voice and copy rules

Write like a knowledgeable host: personal but not overly familiar, expert but not boastful, evocative but not vague, premium but not pretentious, locally grounded but not stereotyped.

Prefer: `journey`, `itinerary`, `tailor-made`, `thoughtfully planned`, `private journey`, `local knowledge`.

Avoid: “untouched Africa,” “tribal experience,” “bucket-list beasts,” guaranteed wildlife claims, fake scarcity, unsupported superlatives, and language that treats communities as attractions.

**One-line description:** Tailor-made Kenya safaris, thoughtfully planned by people who call Kenya home.

## 14. Destination directions

### Maasai Mara

- **Positioning:** iconic open savannah; anchor for first or return safaris.
- **Hero:** Where the grasslands seem to meet the sky.
- **Highlights:** open grasslands, predators, seasonal migration activity, sunrise and sunset drives.
- Explain that migration movement and crossings are seasonal and never guaranteed.

### Amboseli

- **Positioning:** elephant country, wetlands, open scale, and views toward Kilimanjaro.
- **Hero:** Big skies, elephant country, and the mountain beyond.
- Mountain views depend on cloud conditions; do not guarantee them.

### Tsavo

- **Positioning:** a vast, rugged region with contrasting eastern and western landscapes.
- **Hero:** A wilder horizon, written in red earth and open distance.
- Create distinct Tsavo East and Tsavo West subsections.

### Nairobi National Park

- **Positioning:** meaningful wildlife experience beside the capital; ideal for short stays and opening/closing days.
- **Hero:** Wild Kenya, at the edge of the city.
- Do not present it merely as a layover filler.

### Lake Nakuru

- **Positioning:** Rift Valley lake, woodland, escarpment, birdlife, and rhino conservation.
- **Hero:** Lake, woodland, and the changing light of the Rift Valley.
- Do not promise permanent flamingo concentrations.

### Samburu

- **Positioning:** northern dry-country landscape shaped by the Ewaso Nyiro River and regionally distinctive wildlife.
- **Hero:** North of the familiar, the landscape changes its language.
- Describe communities respectfully; do not present people as attractions.
