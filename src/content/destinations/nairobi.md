### Overview

Many travellers treat Nairobi as an airport with a hotel attached. That can mean missing one of the easiest additions to a Kenya itinerary. Nairobi National Park sits on the city's southern edge, allowing visitors to watch rhino and lion with office towers on the horizon. A well-planned day in the city works especially well at the beginning or end of a trip, when a full day of driving may not be practical.

### Landscape

Open plains and acacia woodland on the city's southern edge, with the Nairobi skyline behind them.

### Wildlife

Nairobi National Park holds black and white rhino, lion, giraffe, buffalo, zebra, and cheetah. There are no elephants in the park.

### A full day in Nairobi

The order below follows how the day actually runs. The Sheldrick Wildlife Trust holds a single public viewing hour each day, at around 11:00 a.m., and requires advance booking — that visit is the fixed point the rest of the day is arranged around.

1. **Early morning — Nairobi National Park.** A half-day game drive. The park is best before the day becomes warmer and busier.
2. **Late morning — Sheldrick Wildlife Trust.** The orphaned elephants, during the daily public viewing hour.
3. **Midday — Giraffe Centre.** Rothschild's giraffe at eye level from the raised platform, a short drive from Sheldrick.
4. **Afternoon — Mamba Village or Bomas of Kenya.** Either the crocodile park, or a programme of traditional dance and music representing communities from across Kenya.
5. **Evening — dinner at Carnivore.**

### When to go, and why

Nairobi works year-round. Its elevation keeps the climate relatively mild, though June and July can be cool, overcast, and occasionally wet.

### Sample experiences

The full day above on arrival, followed by a flight to the Maasai Mara the next morning. Alternatively, a shorter combination of the national park and Sheldrick before an evening departure.
