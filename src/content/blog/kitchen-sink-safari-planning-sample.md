## Start with the shape of the journey

A good safari plan begins with pace, not just park names. Travelers often arrive with a list of places they have heard about, but the better question is how the days should feel once they are in Kenya.

### A simple planning sequence

Use this order when turning a first idea into an itinerary:

1. Confirm the month, trip length, and arrival city.
2. Decide how much movement feels right.
3. Choose the anchor landscape.
   - Maasai Mara for open savannah and classic first-safari scale.
   - Amboseli for elephant country and wide skies.
   - Samburu for northern dry-country landscape and a different rhythm.
4. Add one supporting region if the trip length allows it.

#### Notes for families and private groups

- Keep the first full day gentle.
- Avoid changing camps too often.
- Ask about:
  - drive times between regions
  - room layouts
  - meal flexibility
  - age guidance for game drives
